<p><p>Travelling Wave Fault Locators (TWFL) are advanced systems used in power transmission networks to quickly identify fault locations by analyzing the time it takes for fault-induced waves to travel along transmission lines. The market includes three main types: Single-Ended TWFL, which uses measurements from one end of the line; Double-Ended TWFL, utilizing data from both ends for increased accuracy; and Wide Area TWFL, which leverages data from multiple locations for broader fault detection and enhanced situational awareness across vast networks.</p></p>
