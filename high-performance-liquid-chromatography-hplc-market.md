<p><p>High-performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is an advanced analytical technique used for separating, identifying, and quantifying components in a mixture. It employs high pressure to push a solvent containing the sample through a column packed with stationary phase material, facilitating the separation of various analytes based on their chemical properties. HPLC is widely utilized in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, environmental analysis, and food safety testing due to its precision and efficiency.</p><p>The HPLC market is projected to experience significant growth, anticipated to expand at a CAGR of 14.9% during the forecast period. This growth is propelled by increasing demand for advanced analytical techniques in various industries, particularly in drug development and quality control in the pharmaceutical sector. Rising awareness regarding the importance of environmental testing and food safety regulations further contributes to the market's expansion. </p><p>Recent trends indicate a shift toward miniaturization and portable HPLC systems, enhancing accessibility for field applications. The integration of automation and artificial intelligence in chromatographic processes is also gaining traction, leading to improved efficiency and data analysis capabilities.
