<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Central Vascular Access Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PICC</li><li>Centrally Inserted Catheters</li><li>Implanted Ports</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Central Vascular Access Devices (CVADs) include several types designed for long-term intravenous access. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC) are inserted through a peripheral vein and extend to the central venous system, providing stable access for medications and fluids. Centrally Inserted Catheters are placed directly into central veins, ideal for patients needing extended treatment. Implanted Ports are under the skin for infrequent access, offering reduced infection risk. Other devices may include dialysis catheters and specialized access systems for specific patient needs.</p></p>
