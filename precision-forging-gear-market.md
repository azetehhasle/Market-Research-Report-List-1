<p><p>The Precision Forging Gear market is segmented into two main types based on the temperature at which the forging process occurs: Cold Forging and Warm Forging. Cold Forging involves shaping metal at room temperature, resulting in high dimensional accuracy and improved mechanical properties. In contrast, Warm Forging takes place at elevated temperatures, allowing for easier shaping while maintaining structural integrity. Both methods are utilized for producing high-quality gears with enhanced durability and performance, catering to various industrial applications.</p></p>
