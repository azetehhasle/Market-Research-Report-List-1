<p><ul><li>Inactivated Vaccines</li><li>Conjugate Vaccines</li><li>Live/attenuated Vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The antibacterial vaccines market comprises three primary types: inactivated vaccines, conjugate vaccines, and live/attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines use killed bacteria to stimulate an immune response without causing disease. Conjugate vaccines link polysaccharides from bacterial surfaces to proteins, enhancing immune recognition, especially in young children. Live/attenuated vaccines employ weakened bacteria, promoting a robust immune response while mimicking natural infection. Each type serves unique needs in preventing bacterial infections, contributing to public health and vaccination strategies.</p></p>
